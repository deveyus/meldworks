# The Eras of Meldworks (Iterative Design)

## Key to Era Definitions

*   **Description**: A high-level thematic overview of the era's role in the progression. Sets the "mood" (e.g., Survival, Industrial Revolution, Information Age).
*   **Energy Paradigm**: The spectrum of power generation.
    *   *Start:* What the player uses during the bootstrap phase.
    *   *End:* The new form of power unlocked by the era's mastery, which enables the transition to the next era.
*   **Material Paradigm**: The spectrum of material science.
    *   *Start:* The common resources available at the beginning.
    *   *End:* The advanced alloy or synthetic material required to build the next era's machinery.
*   **Logistics**: The *new* transport methods added to the player's toolkit. These are cumulative; a backpack from Era 1 remains useful in Era 10.
*   **Bootstrap**: The initial friction or "grind." The manual tasks required to build the infrastructure that renders them obsolete.
*   **Peak Tech**: The "Paradigm Shift." The technology that ends the Bootstrap phase, shifting gameplay from "struggle" to "management" (e.g., from Scavenging to Farming).
*   **The Crisis**: The systemic bottleneck that occurs when the era's technology is pushed to its limit (e.g., "Bronze bursts under high pressure").
*   **Key Tech**: Specific machines or tools that define the era's gameplay loop.
*   **Key Mods**: The specific mods that provide the core mechanics for this era.
*   **Niche Economies**: Specialized roles (often multiplayer-focused) that exist outside the main industrial progression. May be null in early eras.

**1. Era 1: The Neolithic Era (The Age of Hearth)**
*   **Description:** The struggle against the elements. You are not building an industry; you are building a home. The focus is on warmth, hydration, and securing the first surplus of food.
*   **Energy Paradigm:**
    *   *Start:* Desperation (Running from rain/mobs).
    *   *End:* Muscle Mastery (Efficient Tools, reliable Fire).
*   **Material Paradigm:**
    *   *Start:* Sticks, Flint, Raw Clay.
    *   *End:* **Fired Ceramic** (Pottery/Brick). Durable storage and cooking vessels.
*   **Logistics:** Sacks (bundled items), Clay Vessels (Water/Soup transport).
*   **Bootstrap:** The Subsistence Trap. You are alive, but you cannot scale. Hunting and fishing provide enough for *now*, but not enough to feed the animals or workers required for the next stage of expansion. Foraging fibers for weaving, constructing a "Mud Hut" (Cob/Thatch) to escape the cold, and crafting the first Hammer & Chisel to shape stone.
*   **Peak Tech:** **Temperature Regulation**. The mastery of the Hearth (warmth), the Cellar (food preservation), and Hydration (cooling). This triad stabilizes the player's vitals, allowing for long-term planning.
*   **The Crisis:** Lack of workable materials, you're stuck with clay and rough cut stone.
*   **Key Tech:** Primitive Fishing Rod, Hammer & Chisel, Campfire, Cellar.
*   **Key Mods:** Farmer's Delight, Serene Seasons, Spice of Life, Ceramics
*   **Niche Economies:** None.
