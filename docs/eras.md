**1. Era 1: The Kinetic Era (The Age of Force)**
*   **Description:** This era is primarily about surviving. You start out spending 90% of your time just trying to find a home, scrape together enough food, getting fresh water, and managing temperature. This is the age of sustainable farming—you cannot simply "plop" a field down. Soil quality starts at 10-30% and must be nurtured through crop rotation and mulching. Wild beasts like the Buffalo are not tamed through force, but through sacred dances that "persuade" them into partnership as animals of labor.
*   **Focus:** Rotational Force, Manual Labor, Animal Power, Sustainable Farming (NPK).
*   **Key Tech:** Whim Gin (Horse), Water Strainers, Totemic (Ceremonies / Buffalo Labor), Living Farmland (NPK Logistics).
*   **Key Material:** Copper, Wood, Stone.
*   **Bridge Material:** Bronze

**2. Era 2: The Thermal Era (The Age of Steam)**
*   **Description:** The first industrial revolution. We transition from the muscle of beasts to the expansion of steam (Reciprocating Piston Engines). This era introduces "The Works" cycle: byproducts are no longer waste, but essential precursors.
*   **Bootstrap:** Bronze Boilers (Low Pressure). Relies on Era 1 manual labor to stoke fires; highly inefficient and bulky.
*   **Peak Tech:** **Brass** (Precision Machining). Allows for complex valves, governors, and high-pressure pistons. Introduces the "Maintenance" mechanic (replacing broken Parts).
*   **The Crisis:** The demands of industry outstrip the capacity of wood and charcoal. Bronze cannot contain the pressures required for higher efficiency, and fuel consumption becomes unmanageable.
*   **Bridge Power:** **Coke** (High-Density Fuel). Required to feed the high-temperature demands of the new industry.
*   **Bridge Material:** **Treated Wood** (Byproduct of Coke; yields Creosote). The chemical-resistant foundation required for the vats of Era 3.
*   **Key Tech:** Fire-Tube Boiler, Steam Engines, Coke Ovens.
*   **Key Material:** Iron, Wrought Iron, Treated Wood.


**3. Era 3: The Synthesis Era (The Age of Reactivity)**
*   **Description:** This is an era that mirrors the great era of the chemistry giants like DuPont, making rubber and other things practical.  You're going to need to find mineral and plant sources for things that previously were useless to you. Electricity is starting to show up, as well as the advent of conveyor belts.  Many things are still powered by direct steam or similar.
*   **Focus:** Batch Chemistry, Acids, Rubber.
*   **Key Tech:** The Saltworks (Electrolysis), The Vulcanizer, Resin Tapping.
*   **Key Material:** Rubber, Aluminum, Phenolic Resin (Bakelite).
*   **Bridge Material:** Rubber (Enables the high-speed conveyors of the next era).

**4. Era 4: The Steel Era (The Age of Scale)**
*   **Description:** This is the era where you go from fairly constrained where boilers and vats made up your big things, to truly large scale, many previous machines get much bigger and more efficient.  This is also the era where everything truly updates to have electric versions if it makes any sense. This is the age where trains become widespread.
*   **Focus:** High Voltage, Long-Distance Logistics, Heavy Industry.
*   **Key Tech:** Industrial Blast Furnace, Arc Furnace, Trains.
*   **Key Material:** Steel, Concrete, High Voltage Wires.
*   **Bridge Material:** Reinforced Concrete (The "Vibration Anchor"; required to stabilize the massive, high-pressure machinery of Era 5).

**5. Era 5: The Petrochemical Era (The Age of Plasticity)**
*   **Description:** This era is focused entirely on Oil, both from ground sources, and from things like glass tube algae farms and similar.  Ground oil is simple, but algae or other synthetic options are much more work.  Massive pipelines, tanker cars, and the advent of concrete allows building Via Romana-valid roads.  Power is desperate and limited.
*   **Focus:** Continuous Flow Chemistry, Combustion, Refining.
*   **Key Tech:** Fractionation Towers, Cracking Units, Diesel Engines, Via Romana (Paved Logistics).
*   **Key Material:** Plastic (Polyethylene), Diesel, Lubricant, Asphalt.
*   **Bridge Material:** Lead (Smelted using Sulfur waste from Oil refining; required for Era 6 shielding).

**6. Era 6: The Atomic Era (The Age of Decay)**
*   **Description:** This is that real version of the 'nuclear optimism' you see embodied in Fallout series.  Massive power and refinement as we scale up our power production.  This is the era that solves power.
*   **Focus:** Isotope Breeding, Massive Power Generation.
*   **Key Tech:** Fission Reactors, Flow Batteries (Vanadium Redox).
*   **Key Material:** Uranium, Plutonium, Charged Electrolyte.
*   **Bridge Material:** Monocrystalline Silicon (Produced via Neutron Transmutation Doping in Era 6 reactors).

**7. Era 7: The Information Era (The Age of Data)**
*   **Description:** This is the era where computation comes in, and we start refining old processes kick in, it's also when we first start seriously looking at the stars.
*   **Focus:** Computation, Automation Logic.
*   **Key Tech:** Server Racks, Neural Uplinks, Computer Vision Sawmills.
*   **Key Material:** Circuit Boards, Rare Earth Magnets (Separated via repurposed Era 6 Centrifuges), Gallium Arsenide.
*   **Bridge Material:** Titanium (Lightweight and vacuum-resistant).

**8. Era 8: The Exosphere Era (The Age of Gravity)**
*   **Description:** The peak of this era is landing on the moon and begining the process of processing regolith, this gives the helium-3 required to make fusion power start to kick off, powering the hungry information era compute, and future eras.
*   **Focus:** Escaping the Well, Survival in Vacuum.
*   **Key Tech:** Liquid Fuel Rockets, Oxygen/Thermal Regulation, Soul Anchors.
*   **Key Material:** Moon Dust (Regolith), Space Suits.
*   **Bridge Material:** Graphene (Mass-produced in vacuum environments).

**9. Era 9: The Orbital Era (The Age of Velocity)**
*   **Description:** This era refines the previous, automating rockets, building mass drivers up to Luna --> Earth scale. Represents our true interest in space beyond the local sphere.  Observatories on ground and in space that rival or surpass Aricibo and JWST emerge.
*   **Focus:** Ballistic Logistics, Refinement
*   **Key Tech:** Mass Drivers, Orbital Lasers
*   **Key Material:** Carbon Nanotubes.
*   **Bridge Material:** Advanced Radiators (Required for the heat of the next era's engines).

**10. Era 10: The Interplanetary Era (The Age of Abundance)**
*   **Description:** This era refines the previous, automating rockets, building mass drivers up to Luna --> Earth scale. Represents our true interest in space beyond the local sphere.
*   **Focus:** Gas Giant Exploitation
*   **Key Tech:** Gas Siphons, Antimatter Collection
*   **Key Material:** Metallic Hydrogen, Antimatter.
*   **Bridge Material:** Magnetic Containment Alloys (Required for handling Era 10 fuels and bridging to Era 11 Stellar Lifting).

**11. Era 11: The Transcendent Era (The Age of Mastery)**
*   **Focus:** Macro-Engineering, Stellar-Scale Brute Force.
*   **Key Tech:** Dyson Swarm, Stellar Lifting (Sun-Mining), Von Neumann Construction.
*   **Key Material:** Condensed Matter (Matter structured via extreme gravitational pressure).
